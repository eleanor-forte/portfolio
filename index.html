E-Commerce · Strategy

United Farmers of Alberta — Digital Ecosystem Redesign

UX Designer · Facilitator · Strategic Definer

UFA, one of Canada's largest agricultural cooperatives, needed more than a redesign — they needed alignment between executive vision and customer reality, and a multi-year plan to get there. Starting with ~400 pages of research and stakeholder documentation, I synthesized findings into a single strategic framework and co-created a roadmap built to survive executive scrutiny.

  • 92% task completion rate in RITE testing after Sprint 1
  • Multi-stream sprint tracks to manage design system creation and problem solving
  • Comprehensive content architecture mapping fragmented CMS sources into unified outputs
  • Work oriented around 4 measurable business outcomes: AOV, non-purchase conversions, CLV, retention

AI Integration · Workshop Design

Travel Alberta — Getting AI Online

UXD · Workshop Designer · Lead Facilitator

Travel Alberta wanted to integrate AI meaningfully — but lacked defined problem statements, validated user needs, and cross-departmental alignment. Internal teams ranged widely in technical fluency and some held genuine hesitancy rooted in uncertainty. I designed and led a full-day co-creation workshop that moved participants from curiosity to clarity, resulting in executive-ready concepts and a live shipped product.

  • Pre-workshop survey surfaced fear of redundancy, automation hype, and real process pain points
  • Assumption-flipping exercises broke down emotional barriers before ideation
  • Concepts made executive-ready and plugged into TA's AI roadmap
  • Led to launch of Travel Alberta's first AI tool on December 1, 2025

UX Research · Systems Design

Travel Alberta — Constellation DMO Discovery

UXD · UX Researcher

After a consumer site relaunch, Travel Alberta identified a gap: Constellation DMOs — smaller, often under-resourced regional tourism organizations — weren't engaging with the platform. No user data existed, no requirements were defined, no problem statements validated. Starting from zero, I led the full discovery process: 12 qualitative interviews, goal-based personas, a Jobs to Be Done framework using ODI methods, and two proof-of-concept prototypes that moved the conversation from abstract needs to testable solutions.

  • 12 in-depth interviews across rural one-person operations to multi-mandate teams
  • Goal-based personas representing distinct operational realities across the CDMO network
  • ODI-based JTBD framework prioritized collaboratively with TA stakeholders
  • Secured $300k in funding for a Q3 2026 pilot project

Design Practice

Accessible & Equitable
by default

◈

Standards-Driven from the Start

WCAG 2.0–2.1 AAA and a11y compliance from the earliest wireframes through to production-ready components. Accessibility is embedded early to reduce remediation costs and create more inclusive experiences.

◉

Inclusive Collaboration

Accessibility is a shared responsibility. I work closely with developers, QA specialists, and content teams to ensure compliance is meaningfully integrated — not checked off. This includes facilitating accessibility training for client teams.

◎

Continuous Monitoring

Manual and automated audits combining keyboard-only navigation, screen reader testing (NVDA), and code inspection with Axe Dev Tools and Lighthouse — feeding directly into iterative design and developer QA.
